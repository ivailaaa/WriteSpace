Writing tips

Problems look mighty small from 150 miles up

Posted by Start Bootstrap on August 24, 2023

Getting Started

  • Try to write a little every day, even if it’s only a few lines. Consistency matters more than perfection.
  • Keep a notebook or a simple document where you can jot down thoughts, sentences, or words that catch your attention.
  • Let go of waiting for the “right moment” — starting is often the hardest part.

Overcoming Blocks

  • Set a timer for five or ten minutes and write without stopping, even if it feels awkward at first.
  • Change your environment. A new view can unlock new ideas.
  • Revisit old notes or prompts; sometimes a half-formed idea is the spark you need.

Editing and Revising

  • Read your work out loud. You’ll hear things your eyes might skip over.
  • Be willing to cut. Clear, direct writing is easier to follow and often more powerful.
  • Take breaks between drafts. Fresh eyes will catch what tired eyes miss.

Small Challenges

  • Describe a familiar place using only sounds and smells.
  • Write a scene using no adjectives at all. See what happens.
  • Try rewriting a piece from a different point of view — first person, third person, even second.

Examples to Learn From

Compare flat writing to more vivid alternatives. Notice how small changes bring a scene to life.

  • Flat: She was very sad.
  • Vivid: Her hands trembled as she folded the letter, tears blurring the words she couldn’t bear to read again.
“Start writing, no matter what. The water does not flow until the faucet is turned on.” — Louis L’Amour
“There is no greater agony than bearing an untold story inside you.” — Maya Angelou
